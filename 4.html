SQL Join

SQL joins are the foundation of database management systems, enabling the combination of data from multiple tables based on relationships between columns. Joins allow efficient data retrieval, which is essential for generating meaningful observations and solving complex business queries.

Inner Join

An inner join is the most common type of join in SQL. It returns only the rows where there is a match in both the tables based on the specified join condition. If no matching rows are found, no results will be returned.

Table 1 

    id   |    name    |   city   | commission 
---------+------------+----------+------------
    5001 | James Hoog | New York |       0.15
    5005 | Nail Knite | Paris    |       0.13
    5005 | Pit Alex   | London   |       0.11
    5003 | Lauson Hen | San Jose |       0.12

Table 2 

      id |   cust_name    |    city    | grade | salesman_id 
    -------------+----------------+------------+-------+--------
    3002 | Nick Rimando   | New York   |   100 |        5001
    3007 | Brad Davis     | New York   |   200 |        5001
    3005 | Graham Zusi    | California |   200 |        5002
    3009 | Geoff Cameron  | Berlin     |   100 |        5003

SELECT id, cust_name, city, commission
FROM table1
INNER Join table2
ON table1.id =table2.salesman_id;

Left Join

A left join, also known as a left outer join, returns all rows from the left table (table1) and the matched rows from the right table (table2). If no matching rows are found in the right table, NULL values will be returned.

Table 1 

    id   |    name    |   city   | commission 
---------+------------+----------+------------
    5001 | James Hoog | New York |       0.15
    5005 | Nail Knite | Paris    |       0.13
    5005 | Pit Alex   | London   |       0.11
    5003 | Lauson Hen | San Jose |       0.12

Table 2 

      id |   cust_name    |    city    | grade | salesman_id 
    -------------+----------------+------------+-------+--------
    3002 | Nick Rimando   | New York   |   100 |        5001
    3007 | Brad Davis     | New York   |   200 |        5001
    3005 | Graham Zusi    | California |   200 |        5002
    3009 | Geoff Cameron  | Berlin     |   100 |        5003

SELECT id, cust_name, city, commission
FROM table1
Left Join table2
ON table1.id =table2.salesman_id; 

      id |   cust_name    |    city    | commission | grade

Cross Join

The cross join will display all the rows of both tables.

SELECT id, cust_name, city, commission
FROM table1
CROSS Join table2

Self Join

A Self Join in SQL is a powerful technique that allows one to join a table with itself.

SELECT id, cust_name, city, commission
FROM table1 AS alias1
JOIN table2 AS alias2
ON alias1.column= alias2.column
